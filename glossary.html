Financial Glossary

Demystifying the jargon of Japanese finance.

複利 (Fukuri) - Compound Interest

Interest that is calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest from previous periods. It's often called "interest on interest" and is the engine of wealth growth.

元本 (Ganpon) - Principal

The initial amount of money invested or borrowed, separate from any earnings or interest.

解約返戻金 (Kaiyaku Henreikin) - Cash Surrender Value

The sum of money an insurance company pays to the policyholder if they cancel a life insurance policy before its maturity date or the insured event occurs.

掛け捨て (Kake-sute) - Term / "Throw-away" Insurance

A type of insurance that provides coverage for a specific period (the "term"). There is no savings component or cash value. It's pure protection and therefore much cheaper than savings-type insurance.

NISA (ニーサ) - Nippon Individual Savings Account

A tax-exemption program for investments in Japan. It allows individuals to invest a certain amount each year where the capital gains and dividends are tax-free. Good for flexibility.

iDeCo (イデコ) - Individual-type Defined Contribution Pension Plan

A personal pension plan where contributions are tax-deductible, offering significant tax savings. The funds are invested and grow tax-free, but are locked until age 60.

脱退一時金 (Dattai Ichijikin) - Lump-sum Withdrawal Payment

A one-time refund of pension contributions available to non-Japanese citizens who have paid for at least 6 months and permanently leave Japan.
